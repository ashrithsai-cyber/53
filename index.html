How to train for athletic look
  • Rest 60–90 sec between sets — not more. Keeping rest short burns fat while building muscle.
  • Progressive overload — every week add 1–2 reps OR 2.5kg more weight on at least one exercise
  • Mind-muscle connection — feel the muscle working, don't just move the weight
  • Last 2 reps of every set should feel hard — if not, increase weight
  • Warm up 5 min before every session — light cardio + arm circles + leg swings
  • Never skip legs — legs release the most testosterone, which grows ALL muscles
53-day progression plan
Phase 1 — Foundation Days 1–18
Learn form. Build the habit. Start light.
  • Focus 80% on form — watch YouTube tutorials for each exercise
  • Use moderate weight, 10–12 reps, full range of motion
  • Soreness is normal — push through it
Phase 2 — Build Days 19–38
Increase weight every week. Push harder.
  • Add weight every 5–7 days on compound lifts (bench, squat, deadlift)
  • Drop sets on final set of each exercise — push to failure
  • Body will visibly start changing in this phase
Phase 3 — Define Days 39–53
Lean out. Show the muscle.
  • Slightly reduce calories (200 less per day) to reveal muscle definition
  • Add 10 min HIIT cardio after every session
  • Higher reps (12–15) with shorter rest to cut fat

Golden rules for athletic body
Drink 4 litres of water daily
Muscles are 75% water. Dehydration = weak lifts, slow recovery, bad skin.
Sleep 8 hours — this is when you actually grow
Muscle is built during sleep, not in the gym. No sleep = no results no matter how hard you train.
Consistency over intensity
Showing up every day at 70% beats going 100% twice a week then disappearing.
Don't obsess over the scale
You will gain muscle AND lose fat. Weight may stay same but your body will look completely different.
Take photos every 2 weeks
Progress photos show changes the mirror hides. Day 1, Day 18, Day 36, Day 53.
Eggs are your best friend
Cheapest, best protein source available in India. Eat 6–8 per day minimum.
Warm up. Cool down. Stretch.
One injury can set you back 2–3 weeks. 5 min warm up before, 5 min stretch after — every single session.
